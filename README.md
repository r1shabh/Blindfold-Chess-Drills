# Blindfold-Chess-Drills
Website for chessplayers to improve their skills in blindfold chess via drills

## Drills Provided

Knight's Tour: Your knight starts on the corner of the board and your goal is to move the knight around the board, touching each square exactly once

Attack Vision: You are given a piece and two squares, and you have to answer "yes" or "no" to whether or not the piece is attacking one square from the other. You can see the board if you want as a way to check your work but the purpose of this drill is to do it without looking at the board. Also, it is possible to work out the answer computationally, but in order to improve your chess skill, I recommend you resist the temptation to do that and actually visualize the board in your mind to do this drill

Square Color: kind of a simpler version of the attack vision drill, you are given a square and must determine whether it's a light or dark square. Just like with attack vision, resist the temptation to solve this computationally; make the effort of visualizing the board in your mind.
